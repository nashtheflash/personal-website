# Wyandotte Lake Digital Guide Book
<TLDR
text="There is a decent amount of easy riding out at Wyandotte Lake. I wouldn't say this is a must hit in the area but it is a good time!"
/>

Wyandotte Lake is split, but the damn, into two sides, the east and west. The damn can be crossed in less than 5 minutes so it's not a huge factor. People park everywhere in this park. I don't know what many of them are doing, but mountain bikers normally park at Shelter 14 for the east side and Shelter 9 for the west side. If you have a specific section of trail you want to ride, there is parking nearby. If you want to head straight for the OZ Jump Line, Shelter 11 is probably your best bet!

## Riding
The riding at Wyandotte is pretty easy. If you like hard packed trails with minimal rocks this is a good option! The West side of the park is definatly bigger but the riding on the East side is super fun. The Lake Side Trail is a must hit! The Shouse Pump loop has a cool ending where you ride through the old shit house!

## OZ Jump Trail
I would be careful with this one! The OZ Jump Line runs down a ravine towards the lake. The entire trail is a little rough, but ride able. I am not your dad, but I would walk this before sending it! The wooden features are super rough and, in my opinion, could fall if you rode on them! Ride at your own risk!
<TwoCenteredImages image={[woodOne, woodTwo]} altText={['Columbine Bypass Small Jump', 'Columbine Bypass Big Jump']} titles={['Sketchy First Move', 'Support Not Touching Ground']} width={'h-2/3'} height={'w-2/3'} />

There are some cool dirt jumps. I would bring a shovel if I was serious about hitting them! My honest opinion is to skip this one. I would rather ride [Nall Park][4], [The Ish at Landahl][2] or [Martin City Downhill][3] if I were looking for some bigger jumps!
