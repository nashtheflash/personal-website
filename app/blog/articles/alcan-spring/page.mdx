## Process
Keep in mind that the springs are custom made for your truck. The process is pretty straight forward and emailing the guys over at Alcan Spring directly is easy and they are happy to help! Here is an outline so you know what you are getting yourself into:

### 1. Truck information
You need to have all the information for the truck that you are upfitting!
- Year
- Make
- Model
- VIN

### 2. Weight Your Truck
I wish that I would have gotten my truck weight before installing the Tune M1, but I didn't know how easy it was! Knowing the weight of your truck, with the normal gear inside, is critical for the guys over at the shop to recommend the correct spring. I recommend going to a [CAT Scale][2]. They can be found at almost any truck stop in the United States. Just pull onto the scale and they will give you a sheet that looks like this:

<CenteredImage image={featureImage} altText={'Cedar Niles 3 Corners'} title={'3 Corners'} width={500} height={500} />

What you are looking for is the axle weight — provide that to the guys over at Alcan.

<CenteredImage image={featureImage} altText={'Cedar Niles 3 Corners'} title={'3 Corners'} width={500} height={500} />

### 3. Lift
If your truck has been lifted you should provide that information to Alcan. In my case, the truck had a 1.5" lift. They were able to remove the blocks and have the springs provide the lift instead!

<CenteredImage image={featureImage} altText={'Cedar Niles 3 Corners'} title={'3 Corners'} width={500} height={500} />

# Result
I am really happy with the result. I tried my best to photograph the difference but the pictures really don't do it justice. The following issues went away after getting the new springs:

### Leveling
I could tell an immediate difference when I got in the truck. I didn't even have to move. Just by looking over the hood, I could see that the truck was sitting way flatter.

### Yawing
I was expecting the truck to ride quite a bit rougher, but it was almost the opposite. The truck rides way better — there’s a lot less yawing side to side. It rides straight down the road without tilting side to side.

### Bucking
The bridge over the lake near San Francisco between Half Moon Bay and Livermore is an example of a road that used to cause bucking but no longer does.

### Bouncing
Hitting rolling bumps on the road caused a lot less bouncing that was really noticeable getting on and off bridges. The camper bounce is a lot less just going down the road. Wind seems to affect it less. I feel like it just can’t get into a rhythm because the springs are a lot heavier.

### Summary
I honestly thought that the side effects above were just the result of getting a new camper and that they weren't going anywhere. I was shocked by the result to say the least. I can't find any negative side effects to adding the springs.

# After Care
I wish the team over at Alcan would have let me know that there would be some work required by me after the install. I was lucky because I ended up having the tools needed, but it was just that, luck!

Here are the recommended things to do after install:
- Check the torque on leaf spring bolts after the first 50 miles
- Check the torque AGAIN on leaf spring bolts after another 50 miles
- Torque lug nuts at 250 miles
- Check the torque FINAL time on leaf spring bolts after 500 miles
- Torque lug nuts at 500 miles
- Verify the "Spring Bolts" have not backed off their marks.

Tools:
- Torque Wrench
- Deep well Socket set with big sizes!
- Ratchet

Big and deep sockets are definitely worth mentioning again. The bolts are long, I was lucky to have a pass through socket set, and the biggest socket was the needed 7/8ths. The "Spring Bolts" are another story. The bolts are huge, and I did not have the appropriate socket or adjustable wrench. Lucky, they never backed off their marks.
